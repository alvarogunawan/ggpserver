AAAI 2005 GGP Competition Results:

The first annual AAAI General Game Playing Competition was held on July 10-11, 2005 in Pittsburgh. The winner of the competition, and the $10,000 Stanford Prize was Jim Clune of UCLA and his General Game Player "Cluneplayer".

Qualification Round

The Qualification round held on July 10 consisted of three games. The first (a single-player game) was a version of the common peg-jumping puzzle, sometimes called "Hi-Q". The goal values in this game are determined by the number of pegs remaining on the board when no further jumps are possible. The results of this game were used to seed participants in the main competition round the following day. Competitors also played Criss-Cross, a simplified two-player version of Chinese Checkers, and an additional two-player game called Hallway; both of these were matches against a random player, and were meant to determine whether competitors' systems were able to understand game axioms, make legal moves, and communicate them properly to the game manager.

The axioms for the games used in the Qualification Round (and in the rest of the competition) had all non-GDL reserved words replaced by new, meaningless words, to make human intervention during play much more difficult.

Main Competition

The main competition consisted of three rounds. First, the five qualifiers played two seperate games of 3-way Chinese Checkers. The top two qualfiers (since we only had 5 qualifiers) effectively recieved a bye, playing against a Random player.
  • Round 1, Match 1: Cluneplayer, Goblin, and York: Compround1
  • Round 1, Match 2: Utexas, Fluxplayer, and Random: Compround1
  • Round 1 game axioms: Chinese Checkers (original) Compround1 (competition)
The semifinal round was a variant of Othello-- the corners of the board were removed, and the goal in the game was to finish with as few pieces of your color as possible.
  • Round 2, Match 1: Goblin and Fluxplayer: Compround2
  • Round 2, Match 2: Utexas and Cluneplayer: Compround2
  • Round 2 game axioms: Nothello (original) Compround2 (competition)
The final round was a variant of Corridor. The game (Racetrack Corridor) is a simultaneous move game where players must choose between advancing toward their goal rank, or placing walls to block their opponent's progress.
  • Final: Cluneplayer and Goblin: Compfinal
  • Final game axioms: Racetrack Corridor (original) Compfinal (competition)
